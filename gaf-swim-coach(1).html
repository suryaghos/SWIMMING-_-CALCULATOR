📐 What is Stroke Index?

Stroke Index (SI) = Velocity × Stroke Length. The gold-standard efficiency metric. A higher SI means you travel farther per unit of energy. Coaches use it to track adaptation over weeks. A dropping SI during a set signals fatigue or technique breakdown.

📊 Score (Tracking Sheet)

Score = Time + Stroke Count. Invented by swimming educators as a simple poolside metric for young swimmers. Lower = better. A swimmer improving from Score 36 to 32 has meaningfully improved efficiency — either swam faster, used fewer strokes, or both.

🎯 Benchmarks & Age Groups

Default benchmarks target elite senior swimmers. Selecting Youth (<14) scales all thresholds down ~18–20%. This means a 10-year-old can still score "Excellent" relative to their peer group, keeping ratings motivating and accurate.

⏱️ Time Format

Enter time as MM:SS (e.g. 0:20 for 20 seconds or 1:05 for 65 seconds) or as plain seconds (e.g. 20). Both work.

💡 Coaching Tips

• Track 1–2 swimmers per session deeply rather than all swimmers shallowly.
• Use 25m repeats — easy to measure and compare.
• Share results immediately: "Today 16 strokes, last week 18 — great improvement!"
• Consistency beats complexity. Time + Stroke Count alone puts you ahead of 90% of coaches.
